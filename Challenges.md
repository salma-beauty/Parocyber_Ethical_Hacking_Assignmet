Time Zone Coordination
Collaborating as a group proved challenging due to significant time differences between team members and conflicting personal schedules. Coordinating meeting times and ensuring everyone could contribute simultaneously required careful planning and flexible working arrangements.

HTTPS Configuration Issues
Setting up SSL/TLS on Apache to enable HTTPS scanning on port 443 presented unexpected complications. Despite restarting the Apache service and enabling SSL modules, the Heartbleed vulnerability scan continued to encounter connection issues, requiring additional troubleshooting and configuration adjustments.

Network and Infrastructure Limitations
Intermittent network connectivity issues affected scan reliability and result consistency. These disruptions made it difficult to reproduce results and validate findings across multiple scan attempts.

Knowledge Gaps from Missed Sessions
Some team members missed previous cybersecurity lab sessions, creating knowledge gaps that needed to be bridged. Replicating and understanding prior lab work required additional time and peer to peer knowledge transfer, emphasizing the importance of consistent attendance and active participation.

Key Takeaway:
These challenges reinforced that consistent communication, regular class attendance, and thorough preparation are essential for successful collaborative cybersecurity projects
